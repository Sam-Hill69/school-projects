## Biography

Sir Tim Berners-Lee, born in 1955, is a British computer scientist known for inventing the World Wide Web. Raised in a family of mathematicians and scientists, Berners-Lee developed a fascination with electronics and computers from an early age.

## Important Work

In 1989, while working at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee proposed the idea of a system that would use hypertext to allow researchers to share and update information. This proposal laid the foundation for the World Wide Web, and by 1991, he had developed the first web browser and web server. His creation revolutionized communication, information sharing, and collaboration, shaping the modern internet as we know it.
