## Biography

Linus Torvalds, born in 1969 in Finland, is a software engineer and the creator of the Linux operating system. From an early age, Torvalds exhibited a passion for programming, and his interest in computer science led to the development of one of the most influential open-source projects in the history of computing.

## Important Work

In 1991, while studying at the University of Helsinki, Linus Torvalds released the first version of the Linux kernel, an integral part of the Linux operating system. Linux is known for its stability, security, and open-source nature, making it a popular choice for servers, embedded systems, and as the basis for various other operating systems.
