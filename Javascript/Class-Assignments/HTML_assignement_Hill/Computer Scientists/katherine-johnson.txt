## Biography

Katherine Johnson, born in 1918, was an African-American mathematician who made significant contributions to the fields of mathematics and space exploration. Growing up with an early love for numbers, Johnson pursued a career in mathematics, facing and overcoming racial and gender barriers throughout her life.

## Important Work

Katherine Johnson's groundbreaking work at NASA played a pivotal role in the success of the early space programs. Her precise mathematical calculations were instrumental in the trajectory analysis for the first American in space, Alan Shepard, and the first American orbiting Earth, John Glenn. Johnson's contributions extended to the Apollo moon missions, where her calculations ensured the safe return of the astronauts.
